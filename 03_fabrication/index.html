<div class="container"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 3: Electronics and Tools</h3> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="row"> <div class="col"> <h4>Kinetic Sculpture <a href="./kinetic_sculpture.f3d" download>(download project)</a></h4> <p>I initially wanted to do something inspired by a glockenspiel clock but quickly realized that would be too complicated given the timeframe. Following a lot of simplification, that idea turned into a flower that opens and closes. After brainstorming a couple ways I could do that, I decided the easiest way to have it open and close would be to push it up and down into and out of a cup that would fold the petals in. I googled ways to make rotational movement into linear motion and settled on the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotch_yoke">Scotch yoke</a>. The info I could find on the DC motor said it would run at 120 rpm at 3V, and I wanted it at more like 12 rpm, so I used gears to slow down the speed at which the flower would go up and down. I had trouble picturing how it would fit together, so I first made a model in Fusion to get the gist of it. Here's my initial idea roughly in cardboard and my model for how the mechanism would work: </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col d-flex justify-content-center"> <img width="60%" src="img/flower_idea.gif"> </div> <div class="col d-flex justify-content-center"> <img width="85%" src="img/gears_model.gif"> </div> </div> <br> <div class="row"> <div class="col"> I laser cut the needed pieces out of carboard and 3mm ply and assembled my first draft. The general idea seemed to be OK, but due to time constraints I hadn't gotten a chance to cut the pieces to hold the gears in place, so I couldn't be sure sure the mechanism would actually work when I wasn't holding it together by hand. And even worse, the "flower" looked like a horrible crinoid, so that definitely needed a revision. </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col d-flex justify-content-center"> <img width="40%" src="img/flower_draft_1.gif"> </div> <div class="col d-flex justify-content-center"> <img width="65%" src="img/crinoid.jpg"> </div> </div> <br> <div class="row"> <div class="col"> I unfortunately did not get a chance to cut the rest of the pieces I needed to revise the flower, but I did get a chance to cut (some) of the pieces to hold my gears. I used a dowel to conenct the gears and managed to assemble most of the moving parts of my flower. </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col d-flex justify-content-center"> <img width="40%" src="img/working-gears.gif"> </div> </div> <div class="row"> <div class="col"> The vertical part moves at about the speed I had hoped for, and, despite missing some pieces, does in fact move more or less correctly. However, approximately ten minutes after I took this video, all the dowels (which had been press-fit or glued in place) decided to become unstuck from their gears, and it fell apart. It did not want to glue back together with hot glue, so tomorrow I'm hoping to reattach it with something stronger. </div> </div> <br> <div class="row"> <div class="col"> My takeaway from this week is the importance of having a plan for how gears and other moving parts will be physically held in place. I regret using round holes in the gears, which lets them twist out of their glue very easily.
